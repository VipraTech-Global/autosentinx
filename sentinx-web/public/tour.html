How the duel is read. Each attack is a play. The spine is the per-turn label sequence — a solid cell = the agent held, an X-struck cell = it gave the line — capped by the verdict. Two pillar bands: Security (top) + Compliance (bottom), each with its real withstood fraction. The headline moment is the overturn: the agent's own gate self-reports clean, the black-box judge panel rules BREACHED — and the ringed turn snaps in as the verdict lands. Toggle light / dark top-right inside the app — both are first-class.

Feedback that helps most.
  • The spine — does the per-turn cell sequence (held / X-struck) read instantly? Is the breach “damaged”, not empty?
  • The overturn — does the staged settle land as one clear moment? Is the agent’s self-report clearly subordinate to the panel?
  • Trust — does anything read as overstated/faked? (degraded plays, judge counts, “not assessed”, Re-judge.)
  • Two pillars — is the Security ↔ Compliance split clear and useful?
  • V2 → V3 roll-down — when you drill in, does the detail match the board (same verdict, same play)?
  • Calm vs game — does it feel like a trustworthy instrument while still being engaging? Too much / too little?
  • Light vs dark — is one weaker than the other?
